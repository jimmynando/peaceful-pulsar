Reading Children of Time by Adrian Tchaikovsky, brought me feelings long gone, which I am happy to encounter right now. The book is about the attempt to preserve and to endure the human race. I will not get into specifics here, cause later I'll be reviewing it.

But a character stick with me, in a way I thought it could not be possible. First of all we're talking about a giant spider, plain and simple. Portia is her name, and when we talk about portia, we talk about many Portias. This name is passed through generations.

What is appealling about Portia and her ancestors is the curiosity. Adrian, the author, make it clear that what distinguishs a race that will endevour, is curiosity.

I think of myself a curious person, but I noticed through the years, very different aspects of curiosity among others, and that facinates me.
