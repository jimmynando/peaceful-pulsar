---
title: "Letters I Never Sent"
description: "Thoughts captured in words, left unsent but never forgotten."
date: 2026-03-03
author: Jimmy
---

I have a box of letters that I never sent. Each one folded neatly, sealed with my hesitation and fear of intrusion. They are addressed to people I once knew, people I admired, and people I will never meet again. Writing them was never about sending; it was about creating a space for thoughts too delicate to speak aloud.

I remember one letter in particular, addressed to an old friend who had moved halfway across the world. I wrote about the little things that had changed in my neighborhood, the cafés that had opened and closed, and the way sunlight hit the corners of my apartment in the mornings. I wrote about my regrets and my hopes, and how I sometimes imagined the world they were living in. And then I folded it, sealed it, and put it in the box. It never left my room.

There is a strange comfort in these unsent letters. They are mine to keep, yet they carry the weight of connection I will never fully realize. Sometimes I pull one out, read it aloud, and feel a sense of conversation with a ghost — a memory, a possibility, a moment frozen in time.

Perhaps one day, I will send them. Or perhaps not. Either way, they remind me that communication is not always about reaching the other person; sometimes it is about understanding yourself, capturing fleeting thoughts, and honoring the things you might otherwise forget.
